Political De-Risking: The Unified Development Program

In November 2025, Libya's rival factions signed the "Unified Development Program" — a US-brokered landmark agreement establishing joint nationwide development spending. This unlocked $5 billion in previously frozen funds and empowers the Central Bank and NOC to operate with nationwide coherence.

The UN Security Council has forcefully endorsed the program. For highly risk-averse South Korean financial institutions, this unified budget structure guarantees that multi-billion dollar bilateral agreements will be honored by a cohesive state entity — not fractured regional factions.
